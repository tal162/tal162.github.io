Welcome to my website! I am an assistant professor of Industrial Engineering at the University of Houston. I received my PhD in 2015 in operations research from the University of Toronto. Before joining UH, I was at the department of Computational and Applied Mathematics at Rice University as a postdoctoral researcher.

My general research interests are in the application of optimization techniques such as inverse optimization, robust optimization, and dynamic programming to healthcare operations and medical decision making. Specific application areas that I have recently focused on are radiation therapy treatment planning, organ transplantation, and screening for diabetic complications.

Research Interests
  • Inverse optimization and statistical machine learning
  • Knowledge-based multicriteria optimization in radiation therapy treatment planning
  • Designing telescreening and homecare programs
  • Policy making for organ transplantation centers
